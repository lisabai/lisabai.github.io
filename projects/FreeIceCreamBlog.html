5 Ice Cream Flavors That Will Take You and Your Taste Buds on an Adventure

Looking to travel? Have a sweet tooth? These 5 ice creams from countries around the world will take your taste buds along for the ride.

Written by Lisa Bai on April 30, 2015

Travelling is the best way to experience different cultures and leave your comfort zone. If you are craving a frozen treat while travelling, you're in luck--ice cream is a popular delight around the world. While you are busy navigating your direction and the language, these ice cream flavors will introduce your taste buds to a new world. The following list is based on my experiences both before and during my time while studying abroad in Copenhagen. In each ice cream shop I visit, I go in with an open mind and choose the most exotic flavor on the menu. Either venture into the unknown and grow, or stay home.


1. Tomato & Basil Ice Cream in Nice, France

Tomato and Basil: the classic marriage between two ingredients that pizza and pasta lovers adore. This flavor defies everything thing that one thinks that they know about this frozen treat. Instead of sweetness, the ice cream blends savory and acidic flavors. The texture also emphasizes more iciness than creaminess. Basically, imagine your favorite pasta sauce reincarnated into an ice cream.

Find it at Fenocchio Ice-Cream Parlour in Nice's old town. They boast over 94 classic and innovative flavors of ice creams and sorbets.

2. Salted Licorice Ice Cream in Malmö, Sweden

Salted Licorice is a popular flavor in Scandanavia. It is found in countless products, from candy to liquor. Naturally, it was also the ice cream shop's best-selling flavor. This ice cream is another a slightly savory treat that is extremely creamy. The flavor resembles the dark purple jelly bean most people avoided during Easter, sprinkled with a little hint of sea salt.

Go to Lejonet & Björnen ("The Lion and the Bear"). A friend who grew up in Malmö recommended it.
